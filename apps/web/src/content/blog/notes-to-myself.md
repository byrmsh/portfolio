# Hope

Be hopeful but not delusional. Don't let negativity hinder your potential. Let your only "delusion" be that as long as you do your best, you will succeed. This "delusion" is acceptable because this way of thinking supports itself like a self fulfilling prophecy. Avoid regret as much as possible.

# Enough

Don't get distracted and (more importantly) don't make it easy for yourself to get distracted. Don't try to "challenge your discipline", instead shape your environment in such a way that makes all your tasks require as little discipline as possible. Try to handle tasks in short, focused bursts rather than dedicating a massive time block to one generic thing. Know when to call it "enough" and to step away.

# Memory

Don't ever trust your memory. Write stuff down. Take records of stuff. Document your projects, capture your happy moments, write about your experiences, tend to your loved ones.

# Fairness

The world doesn't owe me anything. Don't get angry at the lack of life's empathy, life doesn't care if you cry or break. You can't affect factors outside of your control, only how you adapt to them. Try to improve your behavior instead of getting angry at life.

# Method

Revise your methods regularly by analyzing their defects and always aim to improve yourself. Don't get static. Always try to take lessons from your own and (more importantly) from other people's experiences. Keep sustainability of your plans and habits in mind and adapt accordingly.

# Meaning

Try to create real value. Don't let yourself get distracted by shallow trends that seem to get rewarded. Aim to be genuinely useful and to improve people's lives. Strive to live your life in such a way that you can face tough questions about yourself, what you do and how you behave without being ashamed or appearing clueless.
